growth

Growth & Product Analytics — SaaS revenue metrics, funnel analysis, cohort retention, and multi-touch marketing attribution

Overview

import vstats.growth // SaaS health metrics mrr := growth.monthly_recurring_revenue([49000.0, 89000.0, 62000.0]) arr := growth.annual_recurring_revenue(mrr) cac_ := growth.cac(120000.0, 200) ltv_ := growth.ltv(240000.0, 3000, 24.0) ratio := growth.ltv_cac_ratio(240000.0, 3000, 24.0, 120000.0, 200) // >3 is healthy ro40 := growth.rule_of_40(0.85, 0.12) // growth + margin // Retention & churn churn := growth.churn_rate(45, 1500) nrr := growth.net_revenue_retention(200000.0, 215000.0, 12000.0, 27000.0) runway := growth.runway_months(500000.0, growth.burn_rate(600000.0, 450000.0, 3)) // Funnel funnel := growth.create_funnel(["Visit", "Trial", "Pay"], [80000, 12000, 1800]) leakage := funnel.funnel_leakage() best_stage := funnel.lowest_drop_off() // Cohort analysis mut ca := growth.create_cohort_analysis( ["Jan", "Feb"], [500, 420], [[500, 310, 210, 140], [420, 260, 170, 110]]) ca.set_cohort_revenue([[0.0, 31000.0, 21000.0, 14000.0], [0.0, 26000.0, 17000.0, 11000.0]]) ltv_proj := ca.ltv_projection(6, 45.0) // Attribution results := growth.linear_attributes( [["paid_search", "email"], ["social", "email", "direct"]], [true, true], [120.0, 95.0]) roi_map := growth.channel_roi(results, {"paid_search": 40.0, "email": 5.0})

The growth module is organized into four source files: metrics.v (SaaS KPIs), funnel.v (conversion funnel analysis), cohort.v (cohort retention and LTV), and attribution.v (multi-touch marketing attribution). All functions are dependency-free and operate on plain V slices and maps.

SaaS Metrics

fn arpa(revenue f64, accounts int) f64

Computes Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA): total revenue divided by the number of accounts. Use ARPA when your pricing is primarily account-based (e.g. per-seat or per-team plans) rather than per-user.

ParameterTypeDescription
revenuef64Total revenue for the period
accountsintNumber of paying accounts in the same period

Returns f64 — revenue / accounts.

fn arpu(revenue f64, users int) f64

Computes Average Revenue Per User (ARPU): total revenue divided by the number of users. ARPU is the basis for LTV calculations and is particularly useful for consumer or usage-based products. Typical monthly ranges: $20–$100 (low-touch / self-serve), $100–$500 (mid-market), $500+ (enterprise).

ParameterTypeDescription
revenuef64Total revenue for the period
usersintNumber of active users in the same period

Returns f64 — revenue / users.

fn cac(acquisition_spend f64, new_customers int) f64

Computes Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): total sales and marketing spend divided by the number of new customers acquired in the same period. The target LTV:CAC ratio is 3:1 or higher — below 1:1 means you spend more to acquire a customer than you will ever earn from them.

ParameterTypeDescription
acquisition_spendf64Total sales and marketing expenditure for the period
new_customersintNumber of new customers acquired in the same period

Returns f64 — acquisition_spend / new_customers.

fn ltv(revenue f64, users int, customer_lifespan f64) f64

Computes Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) as ARPU multiplied by the average customer lifespan — the expected total revenue from a single customer over their entire relationship with the product. customer_lifespan must be in the same time unit as the revenue period (months if revenue is monthly). For a profit-adjusted LTV that accounts for gross margin and steady-state churn, use customer_lifetime_value_simple. The golden LTV:CAC range is 3:1 to 5:1.

ParameterTypeDescription
revenuef64Total revenue for the period
usersintNumber of users in the same period (used to derive ARPU)
customer_lifespanf64Average number of periods a customer remains active

Returns f64 — arpu(revenue, users) × customer_lifespan.

fn ltv_cac_ratio(revenue f64, users int, customer_lifespan f64, acquisition_spend f64, new_customers int) f64

Computes the LTV:CAC ratio: the primary indicator of unit economics health. The golden range is 3:1 to 5:1 — below 3 suggests under-investment in growth or poor retention; above 5 often means you are underinvesting in acquisition. A ratio below 1 means you spend more to acquire a customer than you will ever earn from them.

ParameterTypeDescription
revenuef64Total revenue for the period
usersintNumber of active users (used to derive ARPU)
customer_lifespanf64Average customer lifespan in the same time unit as the revenue period
acquisition_spendf64Total sales and marketing spend
new_customersintNumber of new customers acquired

Returns f64 — ltv(...) / cac(acquisition_spend, new_customers).

fn churn_rate(customers_lost int, total_customers int) f64

Computes the customer churn rate: the fraction of customers who cancelled or failed to renew in a period. Benchmarks vary by segment — monthly rates above 5–7% (low-touch / self-serve), 3–5% (mid-market), or 1–2% (enterprise) signal product-market fit or pricing problems. This measures customer count churn; for revenue churn see net_revenue_retention.

ParameterTypeDescription
customers_lostintNumber of customers who churned during the period
total_customersintTotal customers at the start of the period

Returns f64 — customers_lost / total_customers, in [0, 1].

fn retention_rate(customers_lost int, total_customers int) f64

Computes the customer retention rate: the fraction of customers who remained active during a period. Equivalent to 1 minus the churn rate.

ParameterTypeDescription
customers_lostintNumber of customers who churned during the period
total_customersintTotal customers at the start of the period

Returns f64 — 1 - churn_rate(customers_lost, total_customers), in [0, 1].

fn net_revenue_retention(mrr_start f64, mrr_end f64, churn_mrr f64, expansion_mrr f64) f64

Computes Net Revenue Retention (NRR): how much recurring revenue is retained from existing customers after accounting for expansions, upgrades, contractions, and cancellations. NRR above 100% means upsell and expansion outpace churn — the business grows from its existing base alone. Benchmarks: >120% (world-class), 100–120% (great), 90–100% (good). NRR below 90% indicates a retention problem that no acquisition spend can permanently fix.

ParameterTypeDescription
mrr_startf64MRR from existing customers at the start of the period
mrr_endf64MRR from those same customers at the end of the period
churn_mrrf64MRR lost to cancellations and downgrades
expansion_mrrf64MRR gained from upgrades and expansions of existing customers

Returns f64 — (mrr_start - churn_mrr + expansion_mrr) / mrr_start. Values above 1.0 indicate net expansion.

fn gross_revenue_retention(mrr_start f64, churn_mrr f64) f64

Computes Gross Revenue Retention (GRR): revenue retained from existing customers counting only losses (cancellations and downgrades) — no expansion. GRR is bounded at 100% and measures pure churn impact, making it a conservative floor on retention quality. Benchmarks: >95% (excellent), 90–95% (good), 85–90% (acceptable). Compare GRR against NRR to understand how much of your retention story depends on upsell.

ParameterTypeDescription
mrr_startf64MRR from existing customers at the start of the period
churn_mrrf64MRR lost to cancellations and downgrades

Returns f64 — (mrr_start - churn_mrr) / mrr_start, capped at 1.0.

fn payback_period(cac_ f64, monthly_arpu f64) f64

Computes the CAC payback period: the number of months for a customer's cumulative revenue to recover their acquisition cost. This function uses gross revenue; for a profit-adjusted payback period divide CAC by (ARPU × gross margin) instead. Benchmarks: under 6 months (world-class), 6–12 months (good), 12–18 months (acceptable). Beyond 18 months, cash flow strain typically forces premature fundraising.

ParameterTypeDescription
cac_f64Customer acquisition cost per customer
monthly_arpuf64Average monthly revenue per user

Returns f64 — cac_ / monthly_arpu (months to break even on acquisition).

fn magic_number(net_new_arr f64, gross_margin f64, sales_marketing_spend f64) f64

Computes the SaaS Magic Number: go-to-market efficiency expressed as net new ARR generated per dollar of sales & marketing spend (gross-margin adjusted). Targets: >1.0 (excellent — step on the gas), 0.75–1.0 (efficient — scale cautiously), <0.75 (investigate before increasing spend), <0.5 (fix the go-to-market motion first). The alternative quarterly formula is (Net New MRR × 4) / Prior Quarter S&M Spend.

ParameterTypeDescription
net_new_arrf64Net new Annual Recurring Revenue added in the period
gross_marginf64Gross margin as a fraction in (0, 1); e.g. 0.70 for 70%
sales_marketing_spendf64Total sales and marketing spend in the prior period

Returns f64 — (net_new_arr × gross_margin) / sales_marketing_spend.

fn rule_of_40(growth_rate f64, profit_margin f64) f64

Computes the Rule of 40 score: the sum of a SaaS company's growth rate and profit margin. A score of 40 or above is considered healthy and indicates the company balances growth and profitability. Both inputs should be expressed as percentages (e.g. pass 85.0 for 85% growth, not 0.85).

ParameterTypeDescription
growth_ratef64Annual revenue growth rate as a percentage (e.g. 60.0 for 60%)
profit_marginf64Net or EBITDA profit margin as a percentage (negative values are valid)

Returns f64 — growth_rate + profit_margin. A score ≥ 40 is considered a healthy SaaS business.

fn monthly_recurring_revenue(plan_revenues []f64) f64

Computes Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) as the sum of revenue from all pricing plans. Pass one value per plan tier; the function sums them to produce total MRR. Healthy MRR growth benchmarks: 10–20% monthly (early stage), 5–10% (growth), 3–5% (scale). Prefer MRR for operational tracking; use ARR for investor reporting and valuation once revenue exceeds $1M.

ParameterTypeDescription
plan_revenues[]f64Slice of monthly revenues, one element per pricing plan or tier

Returns f64 — sum of all plan revenues.

fn annual_recurring_revenue(mrr f64) f64

Converts Monthly Recurring Revenue to Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) by multiplying by 12. ARR is the standard metric for valuation, investor reporting, and go-to-market planning. Switch from MRR to ARR as your primary headline metric once revenue exceeds $1M or when communicating with investors and acquirers.

ParameterTypeDescription
mrrf64Monthly Recurring Revenue

Returns f64 — mrr × 12.

fn customer_lifetime_value_simple(arpu f64, gross_margin f64, monthly_churn f64) f64

Computes a profit-adjusted LTV using the steady-state formula for a subscription business with constant churn: (ARPU × gross_margin) / monthly_churn. This is the preferred LTV formula for unit-economics analysis because it measures how much gross profit you actually retain per customer, not just revenue. The result is directly comparable to CAC when targeting the 3:1 to 5:1 golden ratio.

ParameterTypeDescription
arpuf64Average monthly revenue per user
gross_marginf64Gross margin as a fraction in (0, 1); e.g. 0.75 for 75%
monthly_churnf64Monthly churn rate as a fraction in (0, 1); must be > 0

Returns f64 — (arpu × gross_margin) / monthly_churn.

fn burn_rate(starting_cash f64, ending_cash f64, months int) f64

Computes the average net monthly cash burn: how much cash the company consumes per month. A positive result means cash is being spent (net burn); a negative result means cash is being generated (net positive). Use this with runway_months to estimate how long the company can operate without raising more capital.

ParameterTypeDescription
starting_cashf64Cash balance at the start of the period
ending_cashf64Cash balance at the end of the period
monthsintNumber of months in the measurement period

Returns f64 — (starting_cash - ending_cash) / months. Positive means net cash burn per month.

fn runway_months(current_cash f64, monthly_burn f64) f64

Computes how many months of runway remain given current cash and monthly burn rate. Assumes a constant burn rate; in practice revenue growth and burn reduction will extend or compress actual runway.

ParameterTypeDescription
current_cashf64Current cash balance
monthly_burnf64Average monthly net cash burn (positive value)

Returns f64 — current_cash / monthly_burn (months of runway remaining).

fn calculate_growth_metrics(revenue f64, accounts int, users int, acquisition_spend f64, new_customers int, customers_lost int, mrr_start f64, mrr_end f64, churn_mrr f64, expansion_mrr f64, customer_lifespan f64) GrowthMetrics

Computes the full suite of core SaaS metrics in a single call and returns them as a GrowthMetrics struct. Useful for dashboard generation or batch reporting where all inputs are available at once.

ParameterTypeDescription
revenuef64Total revenue for the period
accountsintNumber of paying accounts
usersintNumber of active users
acquisition_spendf64Total sales and marketing spend
new_customersintNew customers acquired in the period
customers_lostintCustomers churned in the period
mrr_startf64MRR from existing customers at period start
mrr_endf64MRR from those same customers at period end
churn_mrrf64MRR lost to churn and downgrades
expansion_mrrf64MRR added from expansions and upgrades
customer_lifespanf64Average customer lifespan in periods

Returns GrowthMetrics — a struct with fields for arpa, arpu, cac, ltv, ltv_cac_ratio, churn_rate, retention_rate, nrr, grr, and payback_period.

Funnel Analysis

fn create_funnel(stage_names []string, stage_users []int) FunnelResult

Builds a FunnelResult from parallel slices of stage names and user counts. The stages are assumed to be ordered from top (widest) to bottom (narrowest). The overall conversion rate is computed as stage_users[last] / stage_users[0].

ParameterTypeDescription
stage_names[]stringOrdered list of stage labels, e.g. ["Visit", "Signup", "Purchase"]
stage_users[]intNumber of users at each stage; must be the same length as stage_names

Returns FunnelResult — struct with stage data and overall total_conversion rate.

fn stage_conversion_rate(from_users int, to_users int) f64

Computes the conversion rate between two adjacent funnel stages: the fraction of users who progressed from one stage to the next.

ParameterTypeDescription
from_usersintUsers entering the stage (the larger number)
to_usersintUsers who completed the stage and progressed

Returns f64 — to_users / from_users, in [0, 1].

fn stage_drop_off_rate(from_users int, to_users int) f64

Computes the drop-off rate between two adjacent funnel stages: the fraction of users who did not progress. Complement of stage_conversion_rate.

ParameterTypeDescription
from_usersintUsers entering the stage
to_usersintUsers who progressed to the next stage

Returns f64 — 1 - stage_conversion_rate(from_users, to_users), in [0, 1].

fn (f FunnelResult) get_conversions() []FunnelConversion

Returns the conversion rate for each consecutive stage pair in the funnel. Each FunnelConversion contains from_name, to_name, and rate. The length of the result is one less than the number of stages.

ParameterTypeDescription
fFunnelResultThe funnel to analyze

Returns []FunnelConversion — per-stage conversion rates in funnel order.

fn (f FunnelResult) highest_drop_off() FunnelConversion

Finds the stage transition with the largest absolute user drop-off. This identifies where the funnel leaks the most users and is typically the highest-leverage optimization target.

ParameterTypeDescription
fFunnelResultThe funnel to analyze

Returns FunnelConversion — the stage pair with the highest drop-off rate.

fn (f FunnelResult) lowest_drop_off() FunnelConversion

Finds the stage transition with the smallest user drop-off. Useful for identifying which stage is already well-optimized or as a baseline for benchmarking other stages.

ParameterTypeDescription
fFunnelResultThe funnel to analyze

Returns FunnelConversion — the stage pair with the lowest drop-off rate.

fn funnel_value(stage_values []f64, stage_users []int) []f64

Computes the expected monetary value at each stage of the funnel by multiplying the per-user value by the number of users at each stage. Useful for understanding where revenue is generated or lost across the funnel.

ParameterTypeDescription
stage_values[]f64Expected value per user at each stage
stage_users[]intNumber of users at each stage; must be the same length as stage_values

Returns []f64 — stage_values[i] × stage_users[i] for each stage.

fn expected_revenue(funnel FunnelResult, stage_revenues []f64) f64

Computes the total expected revenue across all funnel stages by summing the product of stage revenue and stage user count. This represents the maximum revenue attainable if all users at each stage converted at their current rates.

ParameterTypeDescription
funnelFunnelResultThe funnel with user counts per stage
stage_revenues[]f64Revenue per user at each stage; must match funnel stage count

Returns f64 — sum of stage_revenues[i] × stage_users[i] across all stages.

fn ab_test_funnel(funnel_a FunnelResult, funnel_b FunnelResult) int

Compares two funnels by their overall top-to-bottom conversion rate and returns the index of the better-performing one. Use this to decide which funnel variant wins an A/B test based on end-to-end conversion.

ParameterTypeDescription
funnel_aFunnelResultControl or variant A funnel
funnel_bFunnelResultVariant B funnel

Returns int — 0 if funnel_a has higher overall conversion, 1 if funnel_b does.

fn segment_funnel(segment_data map[string][]int) map[string]FunnelResult

Builds a separate funnel for each segment from a map of segment names to stage user counts. Useful for comparing funnel performance across user segments such as traffic source, plan type, or geography.

ParameterTypeDescription
segment_datamap[string][]intMap from segment name to ordered list of stage user counts

Returns map[string]FunnelResult — a FunnelResult per segment key.

fn funnel_leakage(funnel FunnelResult) []int

Computes the absolute number of users lost at each stage transition. Unlike drop-off rate, this gives raw user counts, making it easy to prioritize improvements by actual volume impact.

ParameterTypeDescription
funnelFunnelResultThe funnel to analyze

Returns []int — stage_users[i] - stage_users[i+1] for each consecutive pair; length is one less than the number of stages.

fn projected_conversions(funnel FunnelResult, additional_users int) []int

Projects how many users would reach each funnel stage if additional_users entered at the top, assuming the current stage-to-stage conversion rates remain constant. Useful for forecasting the impact of increased top-of-funnel traffic.

ParameterTypeDescription
funnelFunnelResultThe funnel whose conversion rates are used for projection
additional_usersintHypothetical number of new users entering the top of the funnel

Returns []int — estimated user count at each stage for the additional traffic, same length as funnel stages.

Cohort Analysis

fn create_cohort_analysis(cohort_names []string, initial_sizes []int, retention_data [][]int) CohortAnalysis

Builds a CohortAnalysis from parallel slices of cohort names, initial cohort sizes, and a retention matrix. The retention matrix has one row per cohort and one column per period; each cell is the number of users still active in that cohort at that period. Period 0 is typically the acquisition period with the full cohort size.

ParameterTypeDescription
cohort_names[]stringLabels for each cohort, e.g. ["Jan", "Feb", "Mar"]
initial_sizes[]intNumber of users in each cohort at acquisition; must be the same length as cohort_names
retention_data[][]intRetention matrix (rows = cohorts, cols = periods); each value is the user count still active

Returns CohortAnalysis — struct containing all cohort data, ready for retention and LTV queries.

fn (mut ca CohortAnalysis) set_cohort_revenue(revenue_data [][]f64)

Attaches a revenue matrix to an existing CohortAnalysis. Revenue data must mirror the shape of the retention matrix: one row per cohort, one column per period. This enables LTV projection and revenue-weighted cohort comparisons.

ParameterTypeDescription
camut CohortAnalysisThe cohort analysis to augment with revenue data
revenue_data[][]f64Revenue matrix (rows = cohorts, cols = periods); must match the shape of the retention data

Returns nothing — mutates ca in place.

fn (ca CohortAnalysis) retention_at_period(cohort_index int, period int) f64

Returns the retention rate for a specific cohort at a specific period: the fraction of the original cohort still active at that point in time.

ParameterTypeDescription
cohort_indexintZero-based index into the cohort list
periodintZero-based period index; period 0 is the acquisition period

Returns f64 — retention_data[cohort_index][period] / initial_sizes[cohort_index], in [0, 1].

fn (ca CohortAnalysis) avg_retention_at_period(period int) f64

Computes the average retention rate across all cohorts at a given period. Useful for building a composite retention curve that smooths out cohort-specific noise.

ParameterTypeDescription
periodintZero-based period index to average across cohorts

Returns f64 — mean of retention_at_period(i, period) across all cohort indices i.

fn (ca CohortAnalysis) churn_by_period() []f64

Computes the average churn rate at each period across all cohorts. Period-level churn is the fraction of users who were active in the prior period but are gone by the current period. The result has the same length as the number of retention periods minus one.

ParameterTypeDescription
caCohortAnalysisThe cohort analysis to compute churn from

Returns []f64 — average churn rate at each period (length = number of periods - 1).

fn (ca CohortAnalysis) compare_cohorts(name_a string, name_b string) (f64, f64)

Looks up two cohorts by name and returns their average retention rates across all available periods. Use this to compare the long-run retention quality of cohorts acquired at different times.

ParameterTypeDescription
name_astringName of the first cohort (must match a value in cohort_names)
name_bstringName of the second cohort

Returns (f64, f64) — (avg_retention_a, avg_retention_b); the average retention rate for each named cohort across all periods.

fn (ca CohortAnalysis) ltv_projection(periods_to_project int, avg_revenue_per_user f64) []f64

Projects cumulative LTV per user across future periods using the average retention curve derived from all cohorts. At each future period, the surviving fraction of users is estimated from the average retention at that period, multiplied by the per-user revenue to yield incremental LTV, then accumulated.

ParameterTypeDescription
periods_to_projectintNumber of future periods to project LTV over
avg_revenue_per_userf64Average revenue per retained user per period

Returns []f64 — cumulative LTV per user at each projected period, length equals periods_to_project.

fn (ca CohortAnalysis) cohort_velocity(cohort_name string) int

Returns the number of periods until a named cohort retains fewer than 10% of its original users. A lower velocity means the cohort churns quickly; a higher value indicates a sticky, long-retaining cohort.

ParameterTypeDescription
cohort_namestringName of the cohort to analyze (must match a value in cohort_names)

Returns int — the period index at which retention first drops below 10%, or the total number of periods if 10% retention is never breached.

Attribution

fn first_touch_attributes(customer_channels []string, conversions []bool, revenue []f64) []AttributionResult

Attributes 100% of conversion credit and revenue to the first channel a customer touched. Simple and easy to compute, but ignores all nurturing activity that happened after the first interaction. Best suited for products with short sales cycles where discovery is the primary driver.

ParameterTypeDescription
customer_channels[]stringFirst channel for each customer
conversions[]boolWhether each customer converted; must be the same length as customer_channels
revenue[]f64Revenue from each customer (0 for non-converters); must be the same length

Returns []AttributionResult — one entry per unique channel with total attributed conversions and revenue.

fn last_touch_attributes(customer_channels []string, conversions []bool, revenue []f64) []AttributionResult

Attributes 100% of conversion credit and revenue to the last channel a customer touched before converting. Favors bottom-of-funnel channels such as branded search or direct. Use when closing intent is the primary driver and you want to understand what sealed the deal.

ParameterTypeDescription
customer_channels[]stringLast channel for each customer before conversion
conversions[]boolWhether each customer converted
revenue[]f64Revenue from each customer

Returns []AttributionResult — one entry per unique channel with total attributed conversions and revenue.

fn linear_attributes(customer_touchpoints [][]string, conversions []bool, revenue []f64) []AttributionResult

Distributes conversion credit and revenue equally across all touchpoints a customer interacted with. If a customer touched three channels, each receives 1/3 of the credit. A balanced model that avoids over-crediting any single channel.

ParameterTypeDescription
customer_touchpoints[][]stringOrdered list of all channel touchpoints for each customer
conversions[]boolWhether each customer converted
revenue[]f64Revenue from each customer

Returns []AttributionResult — one entry per unique channel with fractional credited conversions and revenue summed across all customers.

fn time_decay_attributes(customer_touchpoints [][]string, touchpoint_days [][]int, conversions []bool, revenue []f64, half_life f64) []AttributionResult

Attributes credit using an exponential time-decay model: touchpoints closer to conversion receive more credit. The weight of each touchpoint is 2^(-days_before_conversion / half_life), then normalized across all touchpoints for each customer. Suitable for longer sales cycles where recent interactions are more predictive of conversion.

ParameterTypeDescription
customer_touchpoints[][]stringOrdered list of channel touchpoints for each customer
touchpoint_days[][]intDays before conversion for each touchpoint; parallel to customer_touchpoints (0 = day of conversion)
conversions[]boolWhether each customer converted
revenue[]f64Revenue from each customer
half_lifef64Number of days after which a touchpoint's weight is halved; controls how steeply credit drops off over time

Returns []AttributionResult — one entry per unique channel with time-decay-weighted attributed conversions and revenue.

fn position_based_attributes(customer_touchpoints [][]string, conversions []bool, revenue []f64) []AttributionResult

Applies a position-based (U-shaped) attribution model: 40% of credit goes to the first touchpoint, 40% to the last, and the remaining 20% is split equally among all middle touchpoints. This model recognizes both acquisition and closing channels while still crediting middle-of-funnel nurturing.

ParameterTypeDescription
customer_touchpoints[][]stringOrdered list of channel touchpoints for each customer
conversions[]boolWhether each customer converted
revenue[]f64Revenue from each customer

Returns []AttributionResult — one entry per unique channel with position-weighted attributed conversions and revenue.

fn channel_roi(attribution_results []AttributionResult, channel_costs map[string]f64) map[string]f64

Computes ROI for each channel: (attributed_revenue - channel_cost) / channel_cost. Channels without a corresponding entry in channel_costs are omitted from the result. Use this to identify which channels generate profit and which destroy it.

ParameterTypeDescription
attribution_results[]AttributionResultOutput from any attribution function
channel_costsmap[string]f64Map from channel name to total spend on that channel

Returns map[string]f64 — ROI per channel; a value of 1.5 means 150% ROI (returned 2.5x the spend).

fn optimal_channel_mix(channel_performance map[string]f64, total_budget f64) map[string]f64

Allocates a total marketing budget proportionally to each channel's performance score. Channels with higher scores receive a larger share. This is a simple linear allocation; for more sophisticated optimization consider constrained solvers.

ParameterTypeDescription
channel_performancemap[string]f64Map from channel name to performance score (e.g. attributed revenue or ROI)
total_budgetf64Total budget to allocate across channels

Returns map[string]f64 — budget allocation per channel; values sum to total_budget.

fn roas(revenue f64, ad_spend f64) f64

Computes Return on Ad Spend (ROAS): the ratio of revenue generated to the amount spent on advertising for a specific campaign or channel. A ROAS of 4.0 means $4 returned for every $1 spent. Unlike ROI, ROAS does not subtract the cost from the numerator.

ParameterTypeDescription
revenuef64Revenue attributed to the campaign or channel
ad_spendf64Total spend on that campaign or channel

Returns f64 — revenue / ad_spend.

fn blended_roas(total_revenue f64, total_ad_spend f64) f64

Computes portfolio-level blended ROAS across all channels combined. Unlike per-channel ROAS, blended ROAS gives a single efficiency number for the entire marketing program. Useful for executive reporting and overall budget sizing decisions.

ParameterTypeDescription
total_revenuef64Total revenue across all channels and campaigns
total_ad_spendf64Total advertising spend across all channels

Returns f64 — total_revenue / total_ad_spend.

import vstats.growth

fn main() {
    funnel := growth.create_funnel(
        ["Landing", "Signup", "Onboarding", "First Purchase"],
        [120000, 18000, 9000, 1800])

    println("Overall conversion: ${(funnel.total_conversion * 100):.2f}%")
    println("")

    for conv in funnel.get_conversions() {
        println("${conv.from_name} -> ${conv.to_name}: ${(conv.rate * 100):.1f}%")
    }

    worst := funnel.highest_drop_off()
    println("\nBiggest leak: ${worst.from_name} -> ${worst.to_name} (${(worst.rate * 100):.1f}% conversion)")

    leakage := growth.funnel_leakage(funnel)
    println("Absolute drop-offs: ${leakage}")

    // Project impact of doubling top-of-funnel traffic
    projected := growth.projected_conversions(funnel, 120000)
    println("If we add 120k more visitors, projected purchases: ${projected[3]}")
}

Multi-touch attribution and budget allocation

import vstats.growth

fn main() {
    // Each customer's ordered touchpoints
    touchpoints := [
        ["paid_search", "email", "direct"],
        ["social", "email"],
        ["paid_search", "direct"],
        ["social", "paid_search", "email", "direct"],
        ["email", "direct"],
    ]
    conversions := [true, true, false, true, true]
    revenue     := [95.0, 60.0, 0.0, 140.0, 75.0]

    // Compare linear vs position-based attribution
    linear_res   := growth.linear_attributes(touchpoints, conversions, revenue)
    position_res := growth.position_based_attributes(touchpoints, conversions, revenue)

    println("--- Linear Attribution ---")
    for r in linear_res {
        println("${r.channel}: ${r.conversions:.2f} conv, \$${r.revenue:.2f}")
    }

    println("\n--- Position-Based Attribution ---")
    for r in position_res {
        println("${r.channel}: ${r.conversions:.2f} conv, \$${r.revenue:.2f}")
    }

    // Compute ROI and optimal budget
    costs := {"paid_search": 80.0, "social": 40.0, "email": 15.0, "direct": 0.0}
    roi_map := growth.channel_roi(linear_res, costs)
    println("\n--- Channel ROI (linear) ---")
    for ch, roi in roi_map {
        println("${ch}: ${(roi * 100):.0f}%")
    }

    perf := {"paid_search": 4.2, "social": 2.1, "email": 6.8}
    budget := growth.optimal_channel_mix(perf, 10000.0)
    println("\n--- Optimal Budget Allocation (\$10,000) ---")
    for ch, alloc in budget {
        println("${ch}: \$${alloc:.0f}")
    }
}
